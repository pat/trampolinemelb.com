Some of the different methods of communication before, during, and after Trampoline

Communication

Email Discussions

The mailing list is the best place for your online trampoline conversations. If you're not already signed up, then we recommend you do this now and put yourself on the Who's Coming list.

The list is a great place to discuss what you might be thinking of talking about at Trampoline, or to share some interesting concepts or ideas you've come across, or to follow up on in-person discussions after the event.

Twitter

The Twitter hashtag is #trampoline. You can see there is already a fair bit of chatter now, and that's guaranteed to increase as the event draws closer. If you're on Twitter, make sure you use the hashtag to mark all Trampoline-related tweets.

If you're not familiar with Twitter: it is a short-messaging website where people can broadcast what they're doing or thinking. It's a great way to share your thoughts on the session you're currently in, or the day in general. You don't need to sign up to track the buzz around the event and what others are saying though - just use Twitter's search page to find tweets that include the #trampoline hashtag.

Photos

Going by the last event, Flickr seems to be the photo-sharing site of choice. You can find and log photos to the Flickr group, and we recommend you tag your photos with both trampolinemelb (to distinguish between potential Trampolines in other cities) and trampolineday.
