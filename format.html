Trampoline

An overview of the format and approach of the Trampoline unconference

Guidelines

While we like to keep Trampoline as free-flowing and dynamic as possible, we do have some guidelines (influenced by the Open Space philosophy) which we feel help drive the event to be a success.

  • The Law of Two Feet
  • If it’s to be, it’s up to me.
  • Some knowledge helps

The Law of Two Feet

At Trampoline, you can go anywhere you like, at any time. More importantly, don’t feel you've committed to a specific session once you sit down there. If you’re neither learning or contributing, you are encouraged to use your two feet and visit the other sessions.

If it’s to be, it’s up to me

Trampoline is not about achieving great things, nor determining future actions. The event is simply a place and time, where you can meet, share and discuss ideas. Because of this, sessions should not be used as a pitch for action.

This doesn’t mean we don’t want to see great things happen, but you need to be the driving force, not us. Please feel welcome to draw people together to discuss a project, and you can use some of the spaces in DonkeyWheel House for this on the day — just not in the session rooms.

Some knowledge helps

You do not need to be an expert to speak at Trampoline, but some knowledge helps. Please don’t be afraid to speak — indeed, we encourage you to! — but it helps to have at least a little knowledge about your topic.
