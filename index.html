Trampoline

Come and share what you find amazing! Bounce around cross-discipline concepts and thoughts that expand your thinking!

What?

Borrowing from the concepts around BarCamps and other unconferences, Trampoline is an informal day of sharing ideas in Melbourne, Australia. The first occurance happened on the 28th of March, 2009. By popular demand, there is another one coming very soon. We're this close to announcing it.

The goal is to cover subjects that will be appealing across different disciplines. Technical or business-focused, environmental or social, new or old, complex or simple—as long as the ideas are interesting and you find them amazing, they’ll fit right in.

Trampoline is a self-organising event for those who find the world interesting, have something to offer and share, and have an inquisitive mind. All attendees are expected to come along with participation in mind—the discussions (sessions is a bit too formal, lectures even moreso) are suggested and scheduled at the start of the day.

Some examples from the first event:

  • Pieter Peach bounced around some ideas on the future of health
  • Donal O Duibhir waxed lyrical about zen, IT and permaculture
  • Dan Walmsley demonstrated the parallels between improvisational comedy and entrepreneurship
  • Sam Sabey pondered energy efficiency and a distributed community energy future
  • Pete Yandell teased us with a discussion on how to make almost anything

The Full Day's Proceedings

Where?

Trampoline will again be happening at the fantastic Donkey Wheel, at 673 Bourke St in Melbourne's CBD.

How?

We'll be announcing the date and opening up (free) reservations for the next Trampoline sometime in the next month. To hear the news first, sign up on the group discussion email list.

Why?

Because we love inspiring, absorbing conversations, and we love big concepts that make us think differently about the world. We think you might too.
